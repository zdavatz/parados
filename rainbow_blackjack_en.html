RAINBOW BLACKJACK

Colorful 21 — a game by Walter Prossnitz

Two players build 6 colored towers, trying to get as close to 21 as possible — like Blackjack, but with colored stones!

Player 1 gets the primary colors (Red, Blue, Yellow),
Player 2 gets the secondary colors (Green, Orange, Purple).
Each player also has 3 gray joker stones.

Rainbow Blackjack — Rules

Overview

Two players build 6 colored towers together. Goal: Get as close to 21 as possible in each tower. The player who placed the last stone in a tower wins the point for that tower.

Components

  • 36 colored stones: numbers 1–6 in Red, Blue, Yellow, Green, Orange, Purple
  • 6 gray joker stones (3 per player)
  • Player 1: Primary colors (Red, Blue, Yellow) + 3 jokers = 21 stones
  • Player 2: Secondary colors (Green, Orange, Purple) + 3 jokers = 21 stones

Setup

  • 3 random stones from each player are removed face-down from the game.
  • The first 9 stones are arranged face-down in a 3×3 grid.
  • You may look at your own stones but cannot rearrange them.

Gameplay

  • Each turn, pick one row or column (3 stones) from the grid.
  • Before placing, you must announce either the colors or the numbers of the 3 stones (Gray = 0).
  • Then place the 3 stones one at a time in any order into the towers.
  • Colored stones go into the tower matching their color. Gray jokers may go in any tower.

Grid & Rounds

  • Rounds 1–3: Stones from the first 3×3 grid. First row freely choosable, then always the adjacent one.
  • Rounds 4–6: Remaining 9 stones arranged in a new 3×3 grid. First pick from the 4 outer edges (top, bottom, left, right), then adjacent.
  • Once you pick a row, you must stay with rows; once a column, stay with columns.

Turn Order

Round 1: P1 starts, Round 2: P2, Round 3: P1, Round 4: P1, Round 5: P2, Round 6: P1. This gives each player exactly 2 double turns.

Gray Jokers

  • A joker takes the value of the previously placed stone in that tower.
  • A joker cannot be the first stone in a tower.
  • Two jokers cannot be placed consecutively.
  • A joker on top of a tower means no point for that tower.
  • Replacing a joker: If a joker is on top and you have a colored stone that gets closer to 21, but with the joker it would exceed 21, you may remove the joker and place your stone.

Scoring

6 towers = 6 possible points. The player who placed the last stone in a tower gets the point (unless a joker is on top). The player with more points wins.
