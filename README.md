## Share

Every game has a **Share on WhatsApp** button (WhatsApp green `#25D366`) placed to the right of the game title. It opens WhatsApp (or web.whatsapp.com as fallback) in a new tab with a pre-filled message containing the current game URL plus iOS App Store and Google Play links to the Parados app.

## Games

- **DUK — The Impatient Kangaroo** — The plan: to create a 21st century successor to the super-hit solo puzzle game Rushhour. Many players think that hopping through the outback collecting goodies is more fun than trying to shove your way through traffic? Another advantage — thanks to the program, there's all kinds of different ways to play:-) Three difficulty tiers of handcrafted puzzles (Beginner → Intermediate → Advanced) plus "Take a Chance" random mode with three placement zones: Center 6×6 (12 dishes), Inner 8×8 (20 dishes), and Full board (34+ dishes) for endless replay — with color-balance validation ensuring green and blue dishes are well-distributed across the board. A big advantage over Rushhour: Beginner and Advanced puzzles offer two different starting positions (A/B) — same board, different challenge! Intermediate puzzles have a single fixed starting position. Rules button is prominently placed at the very top of the page (above the title) for easy access. **Replay mode:** Load any exported CSV game log to watch the entire game replayed move by move — with play/pause, step forward/back, speed control, and visual highlights showing each dish move and jump. The initial board state is automatically reconstructed from the CSV data.

- **Rainbow Blackjack** — Colorful 21! Two players build 6 colored towers, trying to get as close to 21 as possible — like Blackjack, but with colored stones. The key twist: stones follow the color wheel (Red—Orange—Yellow—Green—Blue—Purple) — each stone can go in its own tower or the two neighboring towers. So Player 1's red stones can reach the purple and orange towers, while Player 2's green stones can reach yellow and blue. Both players compete over every tower! This game is easier to play than to describe:-) Arrange your stones in a grid — they stay face-down and hidden from your opponent, so memorize your positions! Each round, both players pick a row and announce, then alternate placing 1 stone at a time — announce just enough to keep your opponent guessing. Gray jokers add a devious twist...
  - [Deutsch](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/rainbow_blackjack.html) | [English](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/rainbow_blackjack_en.html) | [Remote](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/rainbow_blackjack_remote.html)
- **MAKA LAINA** — It's the first turn and the battle is on! No time to get warmed up in MakaLaina:-) You need to be planning from the get go, evolving your long term strategy — but staying flexible. After each collection, a new disc is drawn from the bag — and YOU choose where to place it in your line of sight. Use this strategically to block your opponent! The disc can't be collected immediately (it's in your LOS), keeping the board count stable for the first 24 turns. After the bag empties, the tempo rises as discs disappear — and every decision carries more weight. Stuck with no valid moves? Your opponent cruelly picks which of your collected discs to sacrifice — then you reposition your collector. Or relinquish your turn — but only 2 times per game! Once you've used them up, you're forced to sacrifice when stuck — keeping the drama alive until the very last move. Secondary colors gain strategic importance as sacrifice buffers. The endgame is where it all comes together...
  - [Play](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/makalaina.html) | [Remote](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/makalaina_remote.html) | [Curated Starting Positions (CSV)](makalaina_starting_positions.csv)
  - 10 curated starting positions transcribed from playtested physical board setups — selectable at game start via setup panel (Random or Position 1–10). Players choose who starts (Black or White) after setup. Color-majority scoring (most colors won wins; tiebreaker: highest single-color score). Leader indicators and winner announcement overlay. At game end, the status bar replaces "GAME OVER" with a persistent winner summary (e.g. "Black wins! 4–2") so players don't need to count colors themselves. Color-aware scoring table: each color name displayed in its actual color, worthless boxes in the disc's own color. CSV export available at any time during play — saves full move history with scores, flags, collector positions, and disc counts. Move history replay at game end — step through every move of the game with navigation controls. Both local and remote versions supported.

## CSV Export

All games with CSV export save files both locally (browser download) and to the server's `csv/` directory. Server-side filenames follow the format `gamename_HHMM_dd.mm.yyyy.csv`. A Python CGI script (`cgi-bin/save_csv.py`) handles the server-side storage.
- **Democracy in Space** — Based on the concept of the US Electoral College (parliamentary systems also have it). Area majority culled to its essence. A gentle opening suddenly transforms into a nail biting race to the finish! The tie breaker condition needs to be kept in mind, but you won't know for a while if you'll need it this time....
  - [Play](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/democracy.html) | [Remote](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/democracy_remote.html)
- **Frankenstein — Where's that green elbow?** — This is even shorter and sweeter than Rainbow. For 1–4 players, it's a "frankly memorable" game (you'll get the pun when you play it). Like most of its colleagues here at Think Ahead, it is so much easier to play online. Age recommendation — 7 years and up. Don't be surprised if the youngest player wins:-)) Each body part color (green, orange, purple, turquoise) has a unique character with real personality — green has wild spiky hair and a crazed bloodshot stare with a stitched scar over one eye and teeth-baring grimace, orange has dense curly hair and an unhinged asymmetric grin with a temple screw, purple sports a mohawk with a stitched scar across the entire face, fangs and a forked snake tongue, blue has a Frankenstein flat-top with sunken dark-ringed eyes, jagged teeth, extra cheek stitches and neck bolts. Ears are split between upper and lower head halves — mix-and-match for hilarious creations with mismatched ears! Head halves connect seamlessly. Anatomical thighs tapering from wide hip to knee with a kneecap arc, and lower legs with a calf muscle bulge tapering to thin ankles are clearly distinguishable from thin straight arms; spread-finger hands and wide flat feet with toe bumps are unmistakable. Clean borderless body tiles for a polished look. **Three build modes:** Classic (torso → outward), Reverse (extremities → inward), or Mixed (one player each direction!) — Reverse starts with 1 hand, 1 foot and 1 upper head pre-placed (instead of torso) — all 4 torso parts go in the deck and you build inward! In Mixed mode, one player builds outward while the other builds inward — a Walter Prossnitz original twist that dramatically increases replayability!
  - [Play](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/frankenstein.html)
- **Divided Loyalties** — Many turns are offensive AND defensive and each one may have long term consequences! It's connect 4, but with 6 colours. Your colour is always loyal to you, your opponent's never is... and the other 4? Sometimes they are, and sometimes they aren't. Tiles can even be loyal in one direction, AND disloyal in another! Not for the faint of heart.... **6 starting positions** numbered in Walter's order (1 Windrad, 2 Raute, 3 Asymmetrisch, 4 Zwei-Säulen, 5 H, 6 Kreuz) — selectable at game start via setup overlay and re-openable via "Neues Spiel" button. Positions are loaded from [`divided_loyalties_starting_positions.csv`](divided_loyalties_starting_positions.csv) at runtime (one CSV row per grid row, each cell its own column c0…c10); edit the CSV to correct distances/shapes and reload — no code change needed. Grid size is computed dynamically per position from the CSV's max x/y (+2 cells of margin for out-of-board stone placement, per game rules). Board pieces are color-coded by size class for instant readability: **S** tokens → black 3×3 with light-gray outer frames, **G** tokens → dark-gray 4×3 with medium-gray frames, **W** tokens → light-gray pyramids (3+2+1) with black frames. Each unique token (S1, S2, W1…) is its own separate board — so two black 3×3s that touch stay two separately-scored regions with a visible seam between them. Background is a muted forest green distinct from the green stone color. Stone palette tuned for balance: orange and violet brighter, green darker (red remains saturated as the "evil" defensive color).
  - [Play](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/divided_loyalties.html) | [Starting positions CSV](divided_loyalties_starting_positions.csv)
- **Capovolto** — The classic game of Othello — on steroids! Add in area control on a random board, numbered discs and a flipping mechanism that is light years ahead of the original, inviting all kinds of devious strategies, and designed to make your brain go all sorts of places it hasn't been before:-)
