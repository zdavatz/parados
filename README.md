## Games

- **DUK — The Impatient Kangaroo** — The plan: to create a 21st century successor to the super-hit solo puzzle game Rushhour. Many players think that hopping through the outback collecting goodies is more fun than trying to shove your way through traffic? Another advantage — thanks to the program, there's all kinds of different ways to play:-) Three difficulty tiers of handcrafted puzzles (Beginner → Intermediate → Advanced) plus "Take a Chance" random mode with three placement zones: Center 6×6 (12 dishes), Inner 8×8 (20 dishes), and Full board (34+ dishes) for endless replay — with color-balance validation ensuring green and blue dishes are well-distributed across the board. A big advantage over Rushhour: Beginner and Advanced puzzles offer two different starting positions (A/B) — same board, different challenge! Intermediate puzzles have a single fixed starting position. Rules button is prominently placed at the very top of the page (above the title) for easy access.

- **Rainbow Blackjack** — Colorful 21! Two players build 6 colored towers, trying to get as close to 21 as possible — like Blackjack, but with colored stones. The key twist: stones follow the color wheel (Red—Orange—Yellow—Green—Blue—Purple) — each stone can go in its own tower or the two neighboring towers. So Player 1's red stones can reach the purple and orange towers, while Player 2's green stones can reach yellow and blue. Both players compete over every tower! This game is easier to play than to describe:-) Arrange your stones in a grid — they stay face-down and hidden from your opponent, so memorize your positions! Each round, both players pick a row and announce, then alternate placing 1 stone at a time — announce just enough to keep your opponent guessing. Gray jokers add a devious twist...

- **MAKA LAINA** — It's the first turn and the battle is on! No time to get warmed up in MakaLaina:-) You need to be planning from the get go, evolving your long term strategy — but staying flexible. After each collection, a new disc is drawn from the bag — and YOU choose where to place it in your line of sight. Use this strategically to block your opponent! The disc can't be collected immediately (it's in your LOS), keeping the board count stable for the first 24 turns. After the bag empties, the tempo rises as discs disappear — and every decision carries more weight. Stuck with no valid moves? Your opponent cruelly picks which of your collected discs to sacrifice — then you reposition your collector. Or just relinquish your turn — the option is always there. The endgame is where it all comes together...
  - [Local](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/makalaina.html) | [Remote](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/makalaina_remote.html) | [Curated Starting Positions (CSV)](makalaina_starting_positions.csv)
  - 10 curated starting positions transcribed from playtested physical board setups — selectable at game start via setup panel (Random or Position 1–10). Players choose who starts (Black or White) after setup. Color-majority scoring (most colors won wins; tiebreaker: highest single-color score). Leader indicators and winner announcement overlay. Color-aware scoring table: each color name displayed in its actual color, worthless boxes in the disc's own color. Move history replay at game end — step through every move of the game with navigation controls. Both local and remote versions supported.
- **Democracy in Space** — Based on the concept of the US Electoral College (parliamentary systems also have it). Area majority culled to its essence. A gentle opening suddenly transforms into a nail biting race to the finish! The tie breaker condition needs to be kept in mind, but you won't know for a while if you'll need it this time....
  - [Play](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/democracy_remote.html)
- **Frankenstein — Where's that green elbow?** — This is even shorter and sweeter than Rainbow. For 1–4 players, it's a "frankly memorable" game (you'll get the pun when you play it). Like most of its colleagues here at Think Ahead, it is so much easier to play online. Age recommendation — 7 years and up. Don't be surprised if the youngest player wins:-))
  - [Play](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/frankenstein.html)
- **Divided Loyalties** — Many turns are offensive AND defensive and each one may have long term consequences! It's connect 4, but with 6 colours. Your colour is always loyal to you, your opponent's never is... and the other 4? Sometimes they are, and sometimes they aren't. Tiles can even be loyal in one direction, AND disloyal in another! Not for the faint of heart....
  - [Play](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/divided_loyalties.html)
- **Capovolto** — The classic game of Othello — on steroids! Add in area control on a random board, numbered discs and a flipping mechanism that is light years ahead of the original, inviting all kinds of devious strategies, and designed to make your brain go all sorts of places it hasn't been before:-)
  - [Play](https://game.ywesee.com/parados/capovolto.html)
